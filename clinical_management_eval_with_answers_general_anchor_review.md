Non-invasive neuromodulation, such as transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS), non-invasive vagal nerve stimulation (nVNS), and external trigeminal nerve stimulation (e-TNS), may provide an alternative for acute migraine management.

## Evidence basis
Randomized controlled trials and systematic reviews of adults with migraine, examining the efficacy and safety of various pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions.

## Evidence summary
The evidence suggests that galcanezumab is associated with a significantly greater reduction in monthly migraine days compared to placebo. Amitriptyline may increase the proportion of patients who experience a 50% or more reduction in monthly migraine days, but the results are far from robust. Corticosteroids, particularly intravenous dexamethasone, may be effective in reducing headache recurrence and improving outcomes in patients with migraine. Non-invasive neuromodulation may provide some efficacy in acute migraine management.

### Answer

## Short answer
The management of hypertension involves the use of antihypertensive medications, lifestyle modifications, and monitoring of blood pressure. First-line therapies include diuretics, beta-blockers, and calcium channel blockers. The optimal target for blood pressure lowering is <130 mmHg systolic. Bedtime dosing of antihypertensive medications is not routinely recommended due to a lack of high-quality evidence.

## Evidence basis
The evidence base for the management of hypertension includes systematic reviews and meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials, observational studies, and mechanistic evidence.

## Evidence summary
The evidence suggests that diuretics and beta-blockers are effective in preventing stroke and congestive heart failure [9042847]. Diuretics also prevent coronary disease and total mortality [9042847]. Calcium channel blockers and angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors reduce blood pressure but have limited evidence for health outcomes [9042847]. Aliskiren, a direct renin inhibitor, has comparable blood pressure-lowering capabilities to angiotensin receptor blockers [21059822]. Cilnidipine, a calcium channel blocker, has similar anti-hypertensive effects to other first-line antihypertensive drugs [34963839]. Bedtime dosing of antihypertensive medications is not routinely recommended due to a lack of high-quality evidence [35983870]. Clinical inertia in the pharmacological management of hypertension can be addressed through physician reminders, ambulatory blood pressure monitoring, and educational interventions [29924011].

## Key papers
